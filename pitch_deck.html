Where This Started
A STORY FROM
THE SLOPES

While working at a ski school in Lebanon, the founder worked alongside Georges Salameh — one of Lebanon's top competitive skiers with Olympic experience at Turin 2006. Despite his credentials, Georges competed commercially with local ski schools as if his Olympic background didn't exist.

Georges Salameh
Olympic Alpine Skier · Turin 2006
"I have the pedigree of being one of the top skiers in the country, with Olympic experience, but in practice I'm competing with local ski schools as if that background doesn't give me any advantage... If people in Lebanon could look up the top skiers in the country and see my name linked to what I offer, it would allow me to position myself properly in the market and charge the premium that reflects my experience."

FERRAN VILASECA

What He Validated

  • Platform concept directly mirrors the creator economy model Kings League is built on — athletes as content channels, not just sport assets
  • Sponsor Scout mode solves a real pain: brands rely on agencies and cold outreach for athlete deals today
  • Three-mode architecture correctly separates stakeholders — the right foundation for a marketplace
  • Intent-signaled outreach (athletes showing openness) is commercially superior to any cold approach

Key Considerations When Scaling

  • Sponsors buy audiences, not athletes — platform must surface follower counts, engagement rates, and platform breakdown (IG vs TikTok vs YouTube)
  • Off-cycle visibility gap: Kings League works because creators already have audiences. How does the platform keep athletes relevant between Games?
  • Offer flow needs deliverable spec — posts, exclusivity windows, usage rights are where real deals are negotiated
  • Local & regional sponsors are an underserved growth angle beyond tier-1 brands
Industry Validation — Olympic Ecosystem & AI Expert
ÍÑIGO CRISTÓBAL LOSADA

Services

What He Validated

  • Agentic AI with structured tool-calling is the right architecture — meaningfully above a generic chatbot for this discovery use case
  • Three-mode design maps cleanly to the stakeholder segmentation the IOC itself recognises
  • Platform addresses a genuine gap: no official infrastructure exists for athlete commercial activity post-Games
  • Data quality pipeline (encoding repair, disambiguation stripping across 11K+ profiles) reflects production-level thinking

Key Considerations When Scaling

  • Athlete opt-in and verification is the credibility gate — athletes must actively consent to commercial listing
  • IOC Rule 40 governs athlete commercial activity during Games windows — a compliance layer is needed for official operation
  • AI accuracy at scale: as athlete count grows, hallucination risk on facts (medals, results, bios) becomes a product liability
  • An official Olympic verification badge would transform platform trust — the difference between a directory and an authorised platform
